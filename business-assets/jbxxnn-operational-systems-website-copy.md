## Problem section
**Section title**
Most growing service businesses do not have a website problem. They have an operations problem that the current tools no longer handle well.

**Intro**
At the beginning, off-the-shelf tools often feel good enough. A booking plugin, a few admin routines, a spreadsheet, and some manual coordination can carry the business for a while.

Then the business grows.

More locations. More staff. More service types. More exceptions. More internal coordination. More work happening outside the system.

That is usually when the real problem starts to show.

**Problem cards**
1. **Scheduling becomes difficult to control**  
   What used to be a simple booking flow becomes harder to manage once multiple locations, staff members, and service types are involved.

2. **The team starts relying on workarounds**  
   Spreadsheets, manual updates, side conversations, and repeated admin tasks begin filling the gaps between tools.

3. **Operations become harder to see clearly**  
   Important information ends up spread across booking software, inboxes, notes, and staff knowledge instead of one dependable workflow.

4. **The business keeps growing, but the system does not**  
   The issue is no longer just customer booking. It becomes staff coordination, operational visibility, and the ability to run the business without constant friction.

## Case study section
**Eyebrow**
Case study

**Headline**
What started as a small website issue became a deeper operations problem.

**Body**
One of my strongest project experiences came from working with a healthcare service business in the Netherlands: **Goud Echo**.

I was not originally brought in to redesign how the business operated. I was hired for a small website issue.

But over time, a bigger pattern became clear.

The business had grown beyond what its original booking setup could comfortably support. What began with an off-the-shelf booking plugin had to serve a more demanding reality:

- multiple locations
- multiple staff members
- multiple service types
- more scheduling complexity
- more operational pressure behind the scenes

At that stage, the real challenge was no longer just the website or booking interface. The challenge was how the business actually worked day to day.

I eventually designed and built a custom operational platform that replaced key parts of the previous workflow and became part of the business’s day-to-day operations.

That experience shaped how I think about service businesses.

The visible issue is not always the real issue. Sometimes the website is simply where the deeper operational friction first becomes noticeable.

**Case study close**
That is the kind of work I am interested in: understanding how the business runs, where the friction is, and what kind of system would genuinely make operations easier.

## Process section
**Section title**
How I work

### Step 1 — Understand the operational reality
We start with a conversation about how the business currently handles enquiries, booking, scheduling, coordination, internal handoffs, and day-to-day administration.

### Step 2 — Identify where the friction actually is
I look beyond the surface symptom to find the underlying process problem — where work is breaking down, becoming manual, or depending too heavily on people remembering what to do.

### Step 3 — Define the right level of system change
Sometimes the right answer is a process improvement. Sometimes it is better structure around the current tools. Sometimes it calls for a custom operational system.

### Step 4 — Design and implement the solution
Once the real problem is clear, I help design and build the system around how the business actually needs to operate.

**Process close**
The goal is not to make the business sound more modern. The goal is to make it easier to run.

## FAQ
### Do we need custom software straight away?
Not always. In some cases, the first step is understanding the operational problem clearly and improving the current workflow before deciding whether a custom system is necessary.

### Can you work with our existing booking software or internal tools?
Yes. I am not attached to replacing everything by default. If the current setup can be improved meaningfully, that is often the best place to start.

### Is this only for healthcare businesses?
No. Healthcare is one strong example, but the broader fit is service businesses with operational complexity — especially where appointments, staff coordination, multiple service types, and multi-location operations create friction.

### Are you building websites or internal systems?
The main focus is operational systems. In some cases the website, booking flow, or customer-facing experience is part of the broader operational problem, but the goal is not a website project for its own sake.

### What kind of businesses benefit most from this?
Usually businesses that are growing steadily and already using several tools, but are starting to feel the limits of generic software, manual admin processes, and disconnected workflows.

### What happens on the discovery call?
We talk through how your operation currently works, where the bottlenecks are, and whether there is a meaningful systems problem worth solving.
